Arrest Statistics of Journalists in Iran Since the Mahsa Amini Movement

By [Author Name], Capital News Service

The death of Mahsa Amini in September 2022 sparked massive protests across Iran, leading to a violent crackdown on journalists who were reporting on the unrest. Thousands of journalists have been arrested, detained, or forced to flee the country.

As the Iranian government continues to censor information about the protests, the international community has focused attention on the arrests and abuse of journalists. These journalists have played a pivotal role in documenting the brutality faced by protestors and in shedding light on the atrocities that continue to unfold in the country.

“Journalism in Iran is really an act of courage,” Jasmin Ramsey, the Deputy Director of the Center for Human Rights in Iran in New York City, told Capital News Service in an interview.
