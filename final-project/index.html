The Fearless Reporting of Elahe Mohammadi
By Tyrah Burris - February 15, 2024

Elahe Mohammadi wanted to write about the funeral for a young woman who died in police custody and to interview the mourning family. The family was planning the funeral of a victim of police brutality, but as soon as Mohammadi arrived, Iranian authorities arrested her.

After her arrest on Sept. 29, 2022, Iranian authorities broke into her house. They seized her personal belongings and later sentenced her to 12 years in prison for spreading “false information,” “propaganda,” and “insulting the leader.” The charges were based on her investigative journalism about protests.

Mohammadi, 36, was charged with acting against Iran’s national security and disturbing public order. She had been covering the protests and arrests in the aftermath of Mahsa Amini’s death. The Iranian authorities were keen on stopping journalists from documenting the unrest that shook the nation.

On Oct. 22, Mohammadi was sentenced to 12 years in prison. She had been an ardent journalist, working for several international publications.

On Jan. 14, Mohammadi was granted temporary release on bail after a global outcry. Despite the difficult conditions, she continued to report, even under the threat of imprisonment.

In 2023 Mohammadi and the other journalist arrested for covering Amini’s death were honored by international human rights organizations for their courage in the face of censorship. Their stories continue to inspire resistance against oppression.
